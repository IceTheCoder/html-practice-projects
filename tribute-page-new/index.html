Steve Jobs Tribute Page

Steve Jobs (24 February 1955, San Francisco, California - 5 October 2011, Palo Alto, California) was a genius American inventor, best known for co-founding Apple; he also co-founded Pixar and founded NeXT. He was a pioneer of the computer revolution in the 1970s and '80s, along with Steve Wozniak.

Timeline

Apple (1976 - 1985)

  • 1 April 1976: Steve Jobs co-founds Apple with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne to sell Wozniak's Apple I PC.
  • 10 June 1977: The Apple II is introduced, one of the first highly-successful mircocomputers. Apple Computer was incorporated the same year.
  • 1978: Steve Jobs is worth $1 million at age 23.
  • 17 May 1978: Steve Jobs's daughter, Lisa Brennan-Jobs is born.
  • 1980: Steve Jobs is worth $250 million at age 25.
  • 1982: Steve Jobs is forced out of the Lisa project.
  • 24 January 1984: Steve Jobs introduces the Macintosh to an enthusiastic audience at Apple's 1984 shareholders meeting.
  • 17 September 1985: Steve Jobs resigns from Apple after a long conflict between the Macintosh, which he supported, and the Apple II, supported by Sculley, the CEO; he starts NeXT, his new venture, with $7 million.

NeXT and Pixar (1985 - 1997)

  • 1986: Steve Jobs funded the spinout of the Graphics Group (later renamed Pixar) from Lucasfilm's computer graphics division with $10 million.
  • 12 October 1988: Steve Jobs introduces the NeXT computer, the first product, with an introductory price of $6,500 (equivalent to $16,100 in 2022)
  • 18 September 1990: The 2nd gen, revised version of the NeXT computer, with an innovative email system, with the ability to share voice, image, graphics, and video. The computer sold poorly - only 50,000 units.
  • 1993: NeXT discontinues its hardware and switches to only producing software.
  • 1994: NeXT reports its first yearly profit, of $1.03 million.
  • 22 November 1995: Steve Jobs is credited as executive producer of Toy Story, the first film produced by Pixar in partnership with Disney.
